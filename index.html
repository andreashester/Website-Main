Andreas Hester - Bassist

About me:

Born in Santa Barbara, California, Andreas Hester was always exposed to deep music culture from childhood by way of his father, and that followed him through his life. Beginning with a bigger drive to learn classical cello outside of elementary school, he quickly developed an interest in the double bass, but not in the same classical styles that he got started with. Instead, he chose to switch over to the jazz space, and later, a wider involvement in almost all the musical endeavors available to him in high school, namely pit orchestras for musicals and percussion theater. In his time attending the California Insitute of the Arts for his undergreaduate degree in Jazz studies, he tries to explore more modern facets of the style, mainly through fusion and freer improv. However, his new main gig is applying this language to his current band, Precipice, a rock group formed in 2022 with CalArts alum Oliva Fries-Farr, which has recently gotten started with shows around the L.A. area.

My ethos:

In not just music, but all art, the boundaries in-between genres has always been a point of interest for me. As a way to identify the broad strokes in a medium, they can be useful in highlighting stylistic qualities and easing people into experiencing new work. However, I also worry that attaching yourself to one place for too long can make moving out feel too overwhelming, especially when the expectations of those around you seem to keep you stuck even more. Many of my favorite works, then, tend to be those with a knack for crossing between these rifts, often merging genres together, and, in turn, I find a lot of inspiration and value in doing so myself. This is not to say that the solution is to ignore definition entirely, lest you end up creating something that feels formless. Rather, it’s through acknowledging these distinctions that one can be given the chance to make something truly unique. Embrace the variety, but don’t be held down in one place. The transition I’ve slowly made from jazz to rock has felt like a test of that flexibility. Currently, while I haven’t composed for Precipice, my current primary focus, our music still straddles those lines already, and I’ve had a lot of freedom to experiment with my language, in turn. I hope to translate that into my own work soon.
